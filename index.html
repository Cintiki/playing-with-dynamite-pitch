The five-part system

Five calls. One shared language.

Each production call gives the audience a practical behavior they can take back to their team.

01

Picture’s Up!

Preparation ends. Team focus begins.

Behavior: Align the room.
02

Rolling!

The room is live. Energy starts moving.

Behavior: Signal the start.
03

Action!

The goal is clear. Work officially begins.

Behavior: Focus the work.
04

Cut!

Pause, evaluate, and adjust without panic.

Behavior: Reset with purpose.
05

That’s a Wrap!

Finish strong. Name the win. Carry the lesson forward.

Behavior: Close the loop.
Deck language

A slide system built for stories, frameworks, and takeaways.

The deck should not feel like one repeated template. It needs a small family of slide types that can carry the rhythm of the talk.

Title Cards

Large, cinematic openings for each major beat.

Story Slides

Behind-the-scenes images, captions, and small production details that make the lesson feel lived-in.

Framework Slides

Simple diagrams and step systems that translate film language into leadership behavior.

Takeaway Slides

Clean closing moments with one useful idea the audience can remember tomorrow.
