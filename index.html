Visual system

Stage polish with county fair soul.

The graphic language has to feel credible in the room while still carrying the oddball charm people remember afterward.

Color

Deep navy, warm cream, carnival red, mustard gold, ribbon blue, paper tan, and ink black.

Type

Bold poster headlines, clean keynote body copy, production labels, and small handwritten accents.

Props

Clapperboards, crew badges, fair ribbons, sticky notes, old photos, tape, and the Big Ideas dynamite metaphor.

Motion

Title-card cuts, production-call transitions, lower thirds, and graphic cues for the highlight reel.

This is the direction I’d build from.

A keynote system that feels clear enough for executives, cinematic enough for the stage, and strange enough to be remembered after the room clears.
